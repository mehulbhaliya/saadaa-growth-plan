Why this cart leaks intent

N1"Estimated Total" manufactures doubt

The PDP states "₹2,199 inclusive of all taxes" (certain). The cart downgrades it to "Estimated Total" (uncertain) — the word implies hidden charges are still coming, at the exact moment of commitment.

Hypothesis: replacing "Estimated Total" with "Total · incl. all taxes" reduces pre-checkout anxiety → ↑ cart→checkout rate. Metric: checkout-start rate. Guardrail: completed orders.
N2Savings shown per-item, never summed

Each line shows "50% OFF" but the cart never totals it. Loss-aversion is strongest when quantified: "You save ₹2,998" reframes the spend as a win.

Hypothesis: an aggregate green savings line increases perceived value → ↑ checkout-start, ↓ coupon-hunting exits. Metric: checkout-start rate + off-site coupon search (Clarity rage/exit).
N3No delivery date or shipping clarity

The cart promises "Free Returns / Exchanges / Secure Payments" but never answers the two questions that decide the order: does it ship free, and when does it arrive?

Hypothesis: a pincode-aware "Free shipping · Delivery by <date>" line reduces uncertainty → ↑ conversion, ↓ "where is my order" tickets. Metric: cart→order CR.
N4Zero scarcity at the decision point

The brand shouts "SELLING FAST" in the nav but the cart — peak intent — has no reservation or real per-size stock. (Distinct from F8, which is the clearance page.)

Hypothesis: a soft reservation timer + real low-stock per size increases completion urgency → ↑ same-session checkout. Metric: time-to-checkout, abandonment. Caveat: only show real inventory — fake timers erode the honesty brand.
